MIDFIELD Workshops

2019 MIDFIELD Institute

MIDFIELD Institute
June 3–4, 2019
Purdue University, West Lafayette, Indiana, USA
Contact: Russell Long, ralong@purdue.edu

Description

We are offering the first MIDFIELD Institute on Monday and Tuesday, June 3–4, 2019, in West Lafayette, Indiana. We welcome faculty, staff, and graduate students.

The cost for the MIDFIELD Institute will be $250 including breakfast and lunch both days and a reception on June 3. Participants will be responsible for their travel and hotel arrangements. More details will be available soon. A limited number of travel stipends might be available.

Our learning objectives can be categorized in two broad classes: qualitative and computational. Qualitatively, by the end of the workshop participants should be able to:

  1. Describe the variables available in the MIDFIELD data
  2. Describe how the MIDFIELD data re organized
  3. Describe key principles of effective data visualization
  4. Identify deficiencies of common graph types
  5. List potential resources beyond MIDFIELD that can contribute to answering research questions

Computationally, participants should be able use the midfieldr R package to work with the MIDFIELD dataset to:

  1. Calculate persistence metrics
  2. Produce data tables to address a research question
  3. Graph the metrics
  4. Use data visualization to explore possible stories in the data

As statistician and graph designer John Tukey (1977) said, “The greatest value of a picture is when it forces us to notice what we never expected to see.”
